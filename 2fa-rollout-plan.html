01 · Executive Summary

Why This Can't Wait

Right now, 25 out of 33 Prodezk Google Workspace accounts have no second layer of security. That includes accounts with access to financial data, client records, payroll systems, and company email. A stolen or guessed password is all it takes for an attacker to get in — and they would have full access, indistinguishable from a real employee.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is the single most effective security measure a company can implement. It is free, already built into Google Workspace, and takes less than 5 minutes per person to set up. There is no cost. The only cost is inaction.

⚠️ The risk is not theoretical. Business email compromise (BEC) attacks cost companies an average of $125,000 per incident. In 2024, 83% of successful corporate breaches exploited accounts without multi-factor authentication. Prodezk is exposed today.

02 · What is 2FA?

A Second Lock on the Door

Your password is like the key to your front door. If someone copies that key — through a data breach, a phishing email, or simply by guessing it — they're inside. No alarm, no warning.

Two-Factor Authentication adds a second lock that only your physical phone can open. Even if someone steals your password, they still can't get in without your phone in hand.

🔐

Here's how it works in practice:

You log in to your Prodezk Google account as usual. After entering your password, Google sends a 6-digit code to your phone — either through an app (Google Authenticator) or as a text message. You enter that code, and you're in. The whole thing takes about 10 seconds. That code changes every 30 seconds, so even if someone intercepted it, it's useless almost immediately.

📱

Two options — you choose what works for you:

Google Authenticator App (Recommended): A free app on your phone that generates codes. Works without cell signal or internet. More secure.

SMS Text Message (Backup option): Google texts you a code every time you log in. Requires cell signal. Easier to start with if you're not comfortable with apps.

✅ Important: You only set this up once. After that, if you're logging in from your usual device, Google may not ask for the code at all — it remembers trusted devices. 2FA mostly activates when someone tries to log in from an unfamiliar device or location.

05 · Setup Guide

How to Set Up 2FA — Step by Step

This guide is written for employees. Share it directly, or include it as an attachment to the Day 1 email.

  • 1

    Go to your Google Account

    Open a browser and go to myaccount.google.com. Make sure you're signed in with your Prodezk email (@prodezk.com).

  • 2

    Click on "Security" in the left menu

    On the left side of the screen, you'll see a list of options. Click on Security.

  • 3

    Find "2-Step Verification" and click "Get Started"

    Scroll down until you see the section called How you sign in to Google. Click on 2-Step Verification, then click the blue Get Started button.

  • 4

    Choose your verification method

    Google will show you options. Choose one of the two methods below (see the options panel for guidance).

  • 5

    Follow the on-screen instructions

    Google will walk you through the rest. For the Authenticator app, you'll scan a QR code with your phone. For SMS, you'll enter your phone number and confirm a test code.

  • 6

    Click "Turn On" to confirm

    Once you've verified a test code, click Turn On. You're done. 2FA is now active on your account. You'll only need to do this once.

📱 Option A — Google Authenticator App (Recommended)

Download the free Google Authenticator app from the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play (Android). It generates a 6-digit code that refreshes every 30 seconds. Works without internet or cell signal. More secure than SMS.

💬 Option B — SMS Text Message (Backup)

Google texts a code to your cell phone number each time you log in. Easier to set up, but requires cell signal. Good backup option if you have trouble with the app. You can always switch to the app later.

💡 Tip for employees: After setup, Google will offer to save "backup codes." Please print or save these somewhere safe — they let you log in if you ever lose your phone. Don't skip this step.
